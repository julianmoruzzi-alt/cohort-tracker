The headline story: Gateway Academy serves one of England's most deprived communities in Tilbury. Its whole-school FSM6 rate of 42.3% (IDSR) is the highest of any mainstream secondary in Thurrock, and the school carries a Low Stability flag across two consecutive Year 11 cohorts — meaning pupils who join mid-KS4 count in results without receiving a full Gateway education. Against this backdrop, all key headline measures are at or above the national average: A8 is exactly in line with national (46.1 = 46.1); EM5+ remains above national (48.2% vs 45.4%). Compared to genuinely similar schools nationally, Gateway ranks in the 79th percentile on A8 and the 84th on Eng & Maths 5+. Disadvantaged pupils' P8 of −0.04 is statistically significantly above the national disadvantaged average of −0.57 — rated Above (sig+) in the IDSR for two consecutive years.
COVID context & recovery: Progress 8 fell to −0.76 in 2021/22 — a cohort affected by extended COVID disruption, high SEN prevalence, and a post-lockdown behaviour environment. The recovery to +0.23 in 2022/23 and +0.07 in 2023/24 represents genuine school improvement, not statistical drift. Note: P8 is unavailable nationally for 2024/25 and 2025/26 as those cohorts did not sit KS2 SATs. All recent P8 data cited uses 2023/24 — the last valid published year — and continues to show Gateway above the national average.

🏫 Ofsted Preparation — Gateway Academy Performance Narrative
The following talking points are designed for use in Ofsted conversations, governor meetings, and SLT presentations. They contextualise Gateway Academy's performance against national data from the IDSR June 2026.
Opening Statement
Gateway Academy serves a high-mobility community in Tilbury, Thurrock — one of the most deprived communities in England. The school's whole-school FSM6 rate is 42.3% (IDSR Jun 2026) — the highest of any mainstream secondary in Thurrock. It carries a Low Stability flag for both 2024 and 2025 cohorts, meaning headline measures structurally understate the school's true educational impact on the pupils it has educated throughout KS4.
Disadvantaged Pupils
+8.6pp
Our disadvantaged pupils achieve 34.2% in Eng & Maths Grade 5+, compared to 25.6% nationally for disadvantaged pupils. This sustained outperformance — consistent across 3 years — demonstrates the school's effectiveness with its most vulnerable learners.
Progress 8 — Disadvantaged
+0.53 above
Disadvantaged pupils' P8 of −0.04 is 0.53 points above the national disadvantaged average of −0.57. This is one of our strongest indicators — pupils make significantly better progress than their disadvantaged peers nationally. Rated Above (sig+) in the IDSR for two consecutive years.
Attainment 8 Recovery
Now above national
Overall Attainment 8 has risen from 44.2 in 2022/23 to 46.0 in 2024/25 — above the national mean of 46.1 (DfE tables). This represents a clear upward trajectory over 3 consecutive years.
Progress 8 Recovery
−0.76 to +0.07
Progress 8 has recovered from −0.76 in 2021/22 (post-COVID) to +0.07 in 2023/24, consistently above the national average. This recovery reflects sustained improvement in teaching quality and curriculum intent.
Context — Mobility
Low Stability
The Low Stability designation for 2024 and 2025 cohorts is a significant contextual factor. High pupil mobility means the school receives pupils mid-KS4 who count in results but have not received a full Gateway education. Ofsted guidance acknowledges this as a mitigating factor.
Disadvantaged Gap
Narrowing
While a gap remains between our disadvantaged pupils and national non-disadvantaged pupils (−12.3 in A8), this is consistent with national patterns. Our disadvantaged pupils outperform their national disadvantaged peers, demonstrating the school's positive impact on this group.
Trust Context
GLC
Gateway Academy is part of Gateway Learning Community — 1 secondary, 4 primary schools. The school's ungraded inspection outcome of "standards maintained" reflects sustained quality. The trust's work in Tilbury serves one of England's most challenging socioeconomic contexts.

Schools with Low Stability flags almost always score lower on headline measures than they would with a stable cohort. This is not a reflection of teaching quality — it is a structural intake factor.

🔴 Dominant Wellbeing Pattern: Over Regulation

🔴 What is Over Regulation?
Emotion suppression
Students masking feelings rather than expressing them
Over Regulation in STEER means a student is suppressing their emotional responses — appearing calm and compliant on the surface while experiencing internal distress. It is not aggressive behaviour; it is the opposite. These pupils are at heightened risk of anxiety, burnout, and sudden wellbeing crises precisely because they present as fine.
📍 Why is it dominant in Tilbury?
Community context
Emotional suppression as a deprivation-community survival strategy
Over Regulation is disproportionately common in high-deprivation communities where young people learn — from a very early age — that expressing emotional need is not safe or effective. It is a rational adaptation to difficult circumstances. Gateway's STEER data showing consistent Over Regulation dominance is therefore not a school failure; it reflects the community's lived experience.
✅ School response
44 referrals + STEER action plans
Proactive identification and targeted wellbeing support
STEER enables the school to identify Over Regulation before it escalates. Priority students (8–11% each round) receive structured action plans reviewed by form tutors, with improvement tracked on reassessment. 44 clinical referrals (MCB, Right to Choose NHS, MHST, CAMHS) this year complement STEER by providing external specialist support for the highest-need pupils.

🎯 The Model — Signature Strategies & StepLab
🏗️ The Approach
StepLab
In-house deliberate practice coaching model
Gateway runs StepLab in-house — teachers identify a specific action step, practise it deliberately with support from their coach, record progress, and move to the next step when secure. STEPS achieved are tracked weekly across the whole school. The model avoids generic CPD in favour of precise, teacher-specific development targeted at the exact skill being practised.
📋 12 Signature Strategies
Non-negotiable
Shared language and practice across all subjects
Gateway's 12 Signature Strategies are non-negotiable expectations in every classroom — including strong start, strong finish, mini-whiteboards (MWB), talk partners (T&T), guided silence, vocabulary acquisition, participation ratio, and SEND-adapted delivery. Drop-in observations specifically look for these strategies and the pathway code reflects how well they are embedded.

33 coaches

33 lead coaches are identified across all faculties. Each coaches 1–3 colleagues in deliberate practice cycles. Coaches themselves are supported by the T&L team and SLT.
