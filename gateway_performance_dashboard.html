🏫 Ofsted Preparation — Gateway Academy Performance Narrative

The following talking points are designed for use in Ofsted conversations, governor meetings, and SLT presentations. They contextualise Gateway Academy's performance against national data from the IDSR November 2025.

Opening Statement
Gateway Academy serves a high-mobility community in Tilbury, Thurrock — one of the most deprived areas in England. The school carries a Low Stability flag in both 2024 and 2025 cohorts, meaning headline measures understate the school's true educational impact on pupils it has educated throughout KS4.
Disadvantaged Pupils
+8.6pp
Our disadvantaged pupils achieve 34.2% in Eng & Maths Grade 5+, compared to 25.6% nationally for disadvantaged pupils. This sustained outperformance — consistent across 3 years — demonstrates the school's effectiveness with its most vulnerable learners.
Progress 8 — Disadvantaged
+0.53 above
Disadvantaged pupils' P8 of -0.04 is 0.53 points above the national disadvantaged average of -0.57. This is one of our strongest indicators — pupils make significantly better progress than their disadvantaged peers nationally.
Attainment 8 Recovery
Now above national
Overall Attainment 8 has risen from 44.2 in 2022/23 to 46.1 in 2024/25 — now above the national mean of 44.4. This represents a clear upward trajectory over 3 consecutive years.
Progress 8 Recovery
-0.76 to +0.07
Progress 8 has recovered from -0.76 in 2021/22 (post-COVID) to +0.07 in 2023/24, consistently above the national average. This recovery reflects sustained improvement in teaching quality and curriculum intent.
Context — Mobility
Low Stability
The Low Stability designation for 2024 and 2025 cohorts is a significant contextual factor. High pupil mobility means the school receives pupils mid-KS4 who count in results but have not received a full Gateway education. Ofsted guidance acknowledges this as a mitigating factor.
Disadvantaged Gap
Narrowing
While a gap remains between our disadvantaged pupils and national non-disadvantaged pupils (-12.3 in A8), this is consistent with national patterns. Our disadvantaged pupils outperform their national disadvantaged peers, demonstrating the school's positive impact on this group.
Trust Context
GLC
Gateway Academy is part of Gateway Learning Community — 1 secondary, 4 primary schools. The school's ungraded inspection outcome of "standards maintained" reflects sustained quality. The trust's work in Tilbury serves one of England's most challenging socioeconomic contexts.

Important: Data Methodology Notes for Fair Comparison
2021/22 — English Literature exclusion: In 2021/22, English Literature was not included in the Attainment 8 calculation for schools that did not enter all pupils, artificially deflating A8 scores that year. This affects Gateway's 2021/22 figure and all comparators equally — but schools with higher Eng Lit entry rates were more affected. Gateway's 2021/22 A8 should be treated as an underestimate.

2024/25 — No Progress 8: P8 is not published for 2024/25 or 2025/26 nationally as the cohort did not sit KS2 SATs (COVID). All P8 comparisons in this dashboard use 2023/24 data — the most recent available for all schools.

Low Stability flag: Gateway carries a Low Stability flag for 2024 and 2025 cohorts. High-mobility schools typically score lower on A8 because late-joining pupils count in results but received less of the school's education. Comparisons should factor this in.

🏫 Pupil Mobility — Ofsted Talking Points

How to contextualise Gateway's mobility profile in Ofsted conversations

Low Stability Explained
Gateway's Low Stability flag reflects the Tilbury community's genuinely transient nature — international relocations, traveller families, asylum seekers, and borough-wide mobility. This is a community characteristic, not a school practice. The school's willingness to absorb in-year admissions is an inclusion strength, not a weakness.
No Off-Rolling
The vast majority of removals from roll are parent-initiated EHE requests or genuine relocations. Permanent exclusions are minimal (2 in 2025/26). Every removal is tracked with reason codes and CME referrals where appropriate. This demonstrates full compliance with statutory duties and no off-rolling practices.
Strong September Intake
210 Year 7 applications for September 2026 — 87% naming Gateway as nearest school, 82% from GLC feeder primaries, 50:50 gender. This demonstrates sustained community confidence and a strong pipeline of well-prepared Year 7 pupils who already know the school's culture.
Improving Stability
Removals from roll have fallen from 75 in 2022/23 to 40 in 2025/26 (to January) — the lowest recorded. In-year admissions are also lower in 2025/26 (22 vs 80 in 2022/23). This suggests the community is stabilising and the school's pastoral systems are retaining pupils more effectively.
