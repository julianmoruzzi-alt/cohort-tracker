The headline story: Gateway Academy serves one of England's most deprived communities in Tilbury. Its whole-school FSM6 rate of 40.6% (IDSR) is the highest of any mainstream secondary in Thurrock, and the school carries a Low Stability flag across two consecutive Year 11 cohorts — meaning pupils who join mid-KS4 count in results without receiving a full Gateway education. Against this backdrop, all key measures are at or above national average. Compared to genuinely similar schools nationally, Gateway ranks in the 79th percentile on A8 and the 84th on Eng & Maths 5+. Disadvantaged pupils' P8 of −0.04 is statistically significantly above the national disadvantaged average of −0.57 — rated Above (sig+) in the IDSR for two consecutive years.
COVID context & recovery: Progress 8 fell to −0.76 in 2021/22 — a cohort affected by extended COVID disruption, high SEN prevalence, and a post-lockdown behaviour environment. The recovery to +0.23 in 2022/23 and +0.07 in 2023/24 represents genuine school improvement, not statistical drift. Note: P8 is unavailable nationally for 2024/25 and 2025/26 as those cohorts did not sit KS2 SATs. All recent P8 data cited uses 2023/24 — the last valid published year — and continues to show Gateway above the national average.

🏫 Ofsted Preparation — Gateway Academy Performance Narrative
The following talking points are designed for use in Ofsted conversations, governor meetings, and SLT presentations. They contextualise Gateway Academy's performance against national data from the IDSR November 2025.
Opening Statement
Gateway Academy serves a high-mobility community in Tilbury, Thurrock — one of the most deprived communities in England. The school's whole-school FSM6 rate is 40.6% (IDSR) — the highest of any mainstream secondary in Thurrock. It carries a Low Stability flag for both 2024 and 2025 cohorts, meaning headline measures structurally understate the school's true educational impact on the pupils it has educated throughout KS4.
Disadvantaged Pupils
+8.6pp
Our disadvantaged pupils achieve 34.2% in Eng & Maths Grade 5+, compared to 25.6% nationally for disadvantaged pupils. This sustained outperformance — consistent across 3 years — demonstrates the school's effectiveness with its most vulnerable learners.
Progress 8 — Disadvantaged
+0.53 above
Disadvantaged pupils' P8 of −0.04 is 0.53 points above the national disadvantaged average of −0.57. This is one of our strongest indicators — pupils make significantly better progress than their disadvantaged peers nationally. Rated Above (sig+) in the IDSR for two consecutive years.
Attainment 8 Recovery
Now above national
Overall Attainment 8 has risen from 44.2 in 2022/23 to 46.0 in 2024/25 — above the national mean of 44.4 (DfE tables). This represents a clear upward trajectory over 3 consecutive years.
Progress 8 Recovery
−0.76 to +0.07
Progress 8 has recovered from −0.76 in 2021/22 (post-COVID) to +0.07 in 2023/24, consistently above the national average. This recovery reflects sustained improvement in teaching quality and curriculum intent.
Context — Mobility
Low Stability
The Low Stability designation for 2024 and 2025 cohorts is a significant contextual factor. High pupil mobility means the school receives pupils mid-KS4 who count in results but have not received a full Gateway education. Ofsted guidance acknowledges this as a mitigating factor.
Disadvantaged Gap
Narrowing
While a gap remains between our disadvantaged pupils and national non-disadvantaged pupils (−12.3 in A8), this is consistent with national patterns. Our disadvantaged pupils outperform their national disadvantaged peers, demonstrating the school's positive impact on this group.
Trust Context
GLC
Gateway Academy is part of Gateway Learning Community — 1 secondary, 4 primary schools. The school's ungraded inspection outcome of "standards maintained" reflects sustained quality. The trust's work in Tilbury serves one of England's most challenging socioeconomic contexts.

Important Context for Inspection
Low Stability (2024 & 2025 cohorts): Gateway carries a Low Stability flag for both cohorts in the IDSR. A significant proportion of the exam cohort joined after the start of KS4. Schools with Low Stability flags almost always score lower on headline measures than they would with a stable cohort. This is not a reflection of teaching quality — it is a structural intake factor.
No P8 for 2024/25 or 2025/26: P8 is not published for these cohorts nationally because pupils did not sit KS2 SATs due to COVID-19. All P8 comparisons use 2023/24 — the most recent year for which valid national P8 is available.
IDSR vs DfE figure note: The IDSR (Ofsted) shows A8 as 46.0; the DfE KS4 performance tables show 46.1. This is a rounding difference between provisional and final data. The IDSR figure (46.0) is used for Ofsted narrative. The DfE figure (46.1) is used for school-to-school comparison in the Similar Schools tab.

🔄 Pupil Mobility — Key Messages for Inspection

The admissions and mobility profile must be understood as a structural feature of Gateway's community context — not a reflection of school quality.

Low Stability flag — cause
Mid-KS4 joiners
The Low Stability flag for 2024 and 2025 cohorts is caused by pupils joining in Years 10 and 11 who count in results but have not received a full Gateway education. This structurally depresses all headline measures.
Not off-rolling
95% parent-led
95% of all departures are parent-initiated or circumstantial (EHE, relocation, left country). Only 5% of removals are permanent exclusions — used only as a last resort after extensive support and intervention.
Absorbing complexity
80 in-year (2022/23)
The school regularly admits asylum seekers, overseas returnees, EHE returners, and pupils with disrupted histories mid-year. It accepts over-number admissions to keep pupils in education. The school absorbs complexity rather than deflecting it.
Community confidence
46% sibling rate
46% of next year's Year 7 have a sibling who attended or attends Gateway. 82% have a GLC or feeder school connection. This is the strongest indicator of community confidence in the school's quality.
Why roll figures vary: Gateway's cohort is genuinely dynamic. Pupils join and leave throughout the year, meaning the on-roll count at any single point differs from IDSR snapshots, DfE census returns, or live MIS data. The IDSR cohort figures (e.g. 197 for Year 11) reflect the exam cohort at census date — not the current live roll. Any comparison between documents should account for these different snapshot dates. The current live roll (June 2026) is approximately 1,037 pupils.

📊 Unifrog — all year groups: Weekly careers activity via Unifrog platform for every tutor group. Job of the week, career exploration, skills profiling and university research — embedded into the school day from Year 7.
💼 Work experience — Year 10: All Year 10 pupils complete a one-week work placement (June 29–July 3, 2026). Supported by CV workshops, letter of application, interview technique, H&S training, and rights & responsibilities sessions.
🎙️ Individual careers interviews — Y11: Every Year 11 pupil receives an individual CIAG interview with the school's careers advisor between September and December, ensuring personalised guidance before applications open.
🏭 Thurrock growth sectors: The careers programme explicitly aligns to Thurrock's economic growth sectors — Health & Care, Engineering & Manufacturing, Finance, Construction, IT & Digital, Creative & Logistics — with Lower Thames Crossing, NHS and DWP as key employer partners.
